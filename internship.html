Internship Training

Objective of the Training Program

Krishna Nursing Home’s Internship Training Program is designed to provide students with strong practical exposure in Psychiatry and related clinical practices. This program aims to:

  1. Equip participants with a clear understanding of the core principles of Psychiatry
  2. Help students develop a structured approach to organizing and identifying clinical cases
  3. Train students in effective therapeutic techniques and meaningful patient engagement
  4. Guide interns on working collaboratively with medical professionals and assisting in accurate clinical assessments

Available Training Formats

To suit different academic requirements, Krishna Nursing Home offers flexible training schedules:

30 Days – Continuous Training
15 Days – Continuous Training
2 Days per Week – Part-time Training

Outcomes of the Training Program

Upon successful completion of the internship, students will be able to:

  1. Build strong rapport and trust with patients
  2. Prepare, document, and organize psychiatric case records efficiently
  3. Reduce nervousness and fear while handling aggressive or violent patients
  4. Choose appropriate strategies for managing patients with different psychiatric diagnoses
  5. Avoid common mistakes typically made by new learners and upcoming psychologists
  6. Demonstrate a confident and professional clinical presence

Curriculum of the Training

The internship program is conducted according to recognized academic curricula and tailored to meet the specific requirements of colleges and universities. Each student receives individual attention and hands-on guidance to ensure effective learning in the field of Psychiatry.
